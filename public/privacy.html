Privacy Policy

Last updated: 06.09.2025

This Privacy Policy explains how Where To? (“we,” “our,” or “us”) collects, uses, and protects your information when you use our website hosted on PythonAnywhere.

1. Information We Collect

When you use our site, we may collect:

  • Comments: Your name, email address, and comment text when you post a comment.
  • Contact Form: Your name, email, and message when you contact us.
  • Social Media Links (Optional): If you choose to share your social media link in a comment, it will be displayed publicly with your comment.

We do not collect passwords, payment information, or sensitive personal data at this stage.

2. How We Use Your Information

We use the information you provide to:

  • Display comments on event pages.
  • Respond to messages sent through the contact form.
  • Allow others to connect with you through optional social media links you share.

We do not sell or rent your information to third parties.

3. Data Storage

  • All information is stored securely in our database on PythonAnywhere.
  • Emails sent through the contact form may also be stored in our email inbox.
  • Comments remain public until we remove them at our discretion (e.g., inappropriate or spam content).

4. Cookies & Tracking

We do not use cookies or analytics tools at this stage.

5. Third-Party Services

Currently, we do not share your information with third parties. If this changes (for example, integrating with social media platforms or payment providers in the future), this policy will be updated.

6. Your Rights

You may:

  • Request that we delete your comments or contact form submissions.
  • Request access to the data we hold about you.

To do so, please contact us at: whereto@web.de

7. Security

We take reasonable steps to protect your information. However, no website or online service is 100% secure. Please avoid posting sensitive information in comments.

8. Changes to this Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. Any updates will be posted on this page with the “Last updated” date.
